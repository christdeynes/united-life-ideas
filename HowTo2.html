How To Change a Flat Tire

  1. Find a safe location to ensure you are not injured and do not cause an accident.
  2. Turn on your hazard lights to alert other drivers.
  3. Apply the parking brake to keepyour vehicle from moving.
  4. Apply tire wedges or another object to further ensure that your vehicle does not move.
  5. Remove any hubcap or wheel cover to expose the lug nuts holding the wheel in place.
  6. Use a tire iron to loosen the lug nuts, but do not completely remove them.
  7. Place a jack under the vehicle. Try to place it under the frame of the car near the tire for the best support.
  8. Pump the jack and lift the car off the ground.
  9. Finish removing the lug nuts.
  10. Pull the flat tire off of the vehicle.
  11. Place the spare tire on the lug bolts by lining up the holes in the rim with the bolts and gently pushing the rim all the way on.
  12. Tighten the lug nuts by hand before lowering the vehicle.
  13. Lower the vehicle most of the way to the ground by releasing the jack and tighten the lug nuts with the tire iron.
  14. Lower the vehicle completely.
  15. Replace any hubcaps or tire covers.
  16. Stow all equipment.
  17. Check the air pressure in the spare tire.
  18. Take the flat tire to a technician. Spare tires aren't made to go long distances or at high speeds, so drive carefully.
